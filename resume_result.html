PROFESSIONAL BACKGROUND

Some employers require finalists for a position to pass a professional background screening. An internal employee or an outside service might perform your screening. Everything you've listed on your resume and in interviews must be true. For example, if you claimed that you managed five people in a previous retail job and you were really just a sales representative like other people on your team, you would be caught in a lie. This likely means you will be dropped from consideration for the job. You also want to include references who will provide positive remarks about your professional background. Always check with references before listing them on your resume. You want to make sure they are comfortable recommending you for a job.
